F.A.Q.'s

Why is blood donation required ?

Blood is a precious fluid in our body, and so far blood cannot be manufactured in laboratories. Blood can only be obtained from individuals (human beings) who donate blood. Blood can be stored only for limited time, therefore there is a need for regular blood donation to ensure that no life is lost due to unavailability of the safe blood.

Is it safe to donate blood / platelets?

Yes, it has been carried out for years now. It is performed in a highly-controlled, sterile environment by professionally trained staff. All the equipments are sterilized and equipment that is in contact with the donor's blood is used only once, eliminating the possibility of any infection during transmission.

What is the eligibility criteria?

Any healthy adult (above 45 kgs), between age 18 - 65 years, mentally alert and physically fit, can donate blood. You can donate blood every 3 months while for platelets that frequency is even higher (once in roughly 20 days). There are some additional criteria related to who can donate blood (specific to diseases, etc)- we recommend you check them in the resources section.

Would your health deteriorate post donation?

No, you have nothing to worry. We just recommend that you drink enough liquids post donation to compensate for the blood donated. Donating blood does not interfere with your ability to perform physically or mentally. However, it is adviced you avoid heavy lifting or strenuous workouts for the rest of the day after the donation. You need to ensure the liquid levels come back to normalcy (roughly takes a day) before starting with those.

Is BloodConnect a registered blood bank?

No, BloodConnect is not a blood bank. We just help keep the government recognized blood banks stocked with blood. We do not collect the blood and keep it with us. We enable these blood banks to have sufficient blood via our camps, helpline. Think of us as the layer connecting donors with blood banks in a more streamlined manner.

Can I reach the BloodConnect office and help out?

While we appreciate the gesture here, honestly we do not have an office. Our volunteers work from their respective homes / offices and we generally conduct our meetings in the common areas in an office / college. Our corporate, college partners help us with these for free and we can do all our regular catch ups there. The registered address just has a store of all our material and our documents. :) You should reach out via this form, we can certainly take forward then.
