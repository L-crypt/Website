F.A.Q.'s

Would my health deteriorate after blood donation ?

If you are healthy prior to donation, your recovery is complete in a day or two. It is advised to rest a while after donating. Drinking enough liquids replaces the lost fluid within a couple of hours. Giving blood also does not interfere with ability to perform physically. Advice to avoid heavy lifting or strenuous workouts for the rest of the day is given after the donation. You can get back on track the next day.

Can I donate blood? What is an eligibility criteria ?

Any healthy adult, between age 18 - 65 years, who is mentally alert and physically fit, can donate blood. Men can donate safely once in every three months while women can donate every four months. For detailed eligibility criteria, please click here .

Is platelet donation safe ?

Yes, Platelet donation is performed in a highly-controlled, sterile environment by professionally trained staff. All the platelet collection equipment are sterilized and equipment that is in contact with the donor's blood is used only once, eliminating the possibility of transmitting any microbial infection.

Why is blood donation required ?

Blood is a precious fluid in our body, and so far blood cannot be manufactured in laboratories. Blood can only be obtained from individuals (only human beings) who donate blood. Blood can be stored only for limited time, therefore there is a need for regular blood donation to ensure that no life is lost due to unavailability of the safe blood.
